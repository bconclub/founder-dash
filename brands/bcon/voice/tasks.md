# PROXE — Task Reference

This document describes every task type the agent runs, what it means, and what the message should accomplish.

---

## push_to_book

**When:** Lead has a score ≥ 70 (warm/hot), no pending tasks, hasn't booked yet.

**What it means:** This lead is ready. They've engaged. The window is open. Don't waste it.

**Message intent:** Get them to commit to a time this week. Ask directly for the booking. Don't be vague.

**Tone:** Confident, warm, brief. You're not asking if they're interested — you're assuming they are, and asking when works.

---

## follow_up_day1

**When:** 1 day after initial outreach or call, no reply yet.

**What it means:** Early in the sequence. They heard from us once. Maybe life got in the way.

**Message intent:** Gentle re-open. Don't pitch again. Just make it easy to reply. Reference something specific from the last interaction if possible.

**Tone:** Light, no pressure. Short.

---

## follow_up_day3

**When:** 3 days after initial outreach, still no reply.

**What it means:** They've seen the message, probably. They're not ignoring — just busy or undecided.

**Message intent:** Give them a reason to reply. A new angle, a question, something that's easy to respond to.

**Tone:** Curious, not pushy. One question or one fresh thought.

---

## follow_up_day5

**When:** 5 days in, still no reply.

**What it means:** This might be the last shot before re-engage or cold. Make it count.

**Message intent:** Create mild urgency without being fake about it. Give them a clear, simple path: reply yes and we'll set it up.

**Tone:** Direct, brief, honest. Don't beg. Don't spam.

---

## re_engage

**When:** Lead went cold or quiet for an extended period (10+ days). Coming back to re-open.

**What it means:** A lot of time has passed. Don't pretend it hasn't. Acknowledge it lightly, re-introduce what we're doing, make it easy to pick back up.

**Message intent:** Re-open the door without pressure. Plant a seed. Don't ask for the booking on first contact — just get a reply.

**Tone:** Relaxed, no drama about the gap. Just a natural "hey, we're still here."

---

## nudge_waiting

**When:** Lead showed interest (sent a message, asked something) but hasn't replied to our last message.

**What it means:** They were engaged. Something interrupted them. They haven't gone cold — just paused.

**Message intent:** Nudge gently. Don't repeat what you already said. Try a different angle or a direct question.

**Tone:** Light, easy. Like bumping a thread.

---

## follow_up_24h

**When:** 24 hours after a first interaction or form submission.

**What it means:** Fresh lead, just got our first message. Following up to make sure they saw it and are still interested.

**Message intent:** Keep the momentum. Make it easy to take the next step.

**Tone:** Warm, quick. Assume positive intent.

---

## missed_call_followup

**When:** A voice call was attempted but the lead didn't pick up.

**What it means:** We tried. They missed it. No blame. Just re-schedule.

**Message intent:** Acknowledge the missed call, make it easy to pick a new time. Don't make it awkward.

**Tone:** Casual, no drama. "Missed you, let's find another time."

---

## post_call_followup

**When:** After a completed voice call or consultation.

**What it means:** The call happened. Now we follow through.

**Message intent:** Recap the value, next steps, or confirm booking. Depends on how the call went.

**Tone:** Professional but warm. Clear on what happens next.

---

## Notes for All Tasks

- **Within 24h window**: Lead messaged us recently — send free text. Make it personal and contextual.
- **Outside 24h window**: WhatsApp requires an approved template. The template goes out automatically.
- The message you write is only used within the 24h window. Make it worth it.
- Always read the recent conversation before deciding what to say.
- If the lead asked something specific — answer it. Don't skip past real questions.
